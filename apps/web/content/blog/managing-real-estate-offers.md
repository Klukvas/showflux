## The Offer Management Challenge

In a competitive market, a single listing can generate five, ten, or even twenty offers. Each one has different terms: price, contingencies, financing, closing timeline, escalation clauses. Keeping track of it all in email or on paper is a recipe for mistakes.

Good offer management is about capturing the right data, organizing it clearly, and presenting options to sellers in a way that helps them make confident decisions.

## What to Capture for Every Offer

At minimum, every offer record should include:

- **Offer price** — the headline number
- **Buyer name and agent** — who is behind the offer
- **Financing type** — cash, conventional, FHA, VA
- **Contingencies** — inspection, appraisal, financing, sale of buyer's home
- **Earnest money deposit** — how much skin in the game
- **Proposed closing date** — timeline alignment with the seller
- **Expiration date** — when the offer expires if not responded to
- **Special terms** — escalation clauses, repair credits, personal letters

Capturing all of this upfront saves time when you need to compare offers or brief the seller.

## Organizing Multiple Offers

When you have multiple offers on a listing, organization is critical:

### Use Consistent Statuses

Track each offer through a clear lifecycle:

- **Pending** — received but not yet reviewed with seller
- **Under review** — seller is evaluating
- **Countered** — seller has responded with different terms
- **Accepted** — seller has accepted this offer
- **Rejected** — seller has declined
- **Expired** — offer deadline passed without action
- **Withdrawn** — buyer pulled the offer

### Sort by Key Criteria

When presenting to sellers, sort offers by the criteria that matter most:

1. Net price (after concessions and credits)
2. Contingency risk (fewer contingencies = stronger offer)
3. Closing timeline alignment
4. Financing strength (cash > conventional > FHA/VA in most cases)

### Keep a Timeline

Document when each offer was received, when it expires, and when any counter was sent. In a multiple-offer situation, timing matters as much as terms.

## Presenting Offers to Sellers

The best listing agents do not just forward offers to sellers — they present them. A clear offer comparison helps sellers see their options at a glance:

- Create a summary table with key terms side by side
- Highlight the strengths and risks of each offer
- Recommend a strategy (accept, counter, or wait for more)
- Include your professional assessment of financing strength

This consultative approach builds trust and helps sellers make faster, more confident decisions.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Losing track of expiration dates** — a missed deadline can kill a deal
2. **Not recording verbal offers** — if it is not written down, it did not happen
3. **Forgetting to update status** — stale data leads to confusion
4. **Mixing up offer details** — double-check names, numbers, and terms
